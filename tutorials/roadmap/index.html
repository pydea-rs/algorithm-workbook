📌 Rules of the week read once, obey daily

🗣️ Narrate aloud, in English Every practice question this week: say your plan out loud before typing. The live interview grades communication as much as code — make thinking-out-loud automatic.
⏲️ The 25-minute rule Stuck for 25 minutes? Read the hint, finish with it, and put the question on your re-do list. Grinding silently is exactly the habit that hurts you in a live interview — practice asking for a nudge instead.
🔁 Keep a re-do list Every miss (failed tests, needed the hint, blew the time budget) goes on a list. Re-solve it from scratch two days later — spaced repetition is what makes it stick.
🐍 One language, all week Pick your interview language now (the course leans Python) and use it for everything. Switching languages mid-week resets your muscle memory.
⏱️ Timers always on Use the per-question timer in the course for every drill. By Thursday, time pressure should feel like normal weather, not a storm.
😴 Sleep beats cramming Memory consolidates during sleep. A tired brain narrating badly loses more points than one unread module ever will. Hard stop every evening.

🕐 How to shape a study day

Morning · ~3h
Read the day's new modules — hardest thinking while you're freshest. Work in 50-minute focus blocks with 10-minute breaks (stand up, look far away).
Midday · 1h+
Real break. Food, a walk, no screens. Your afternoon self will thank you.
Afternoon · ~2.5h
Practice sets + LeetCode, timers on, narrating aloud. This is rehearsal, not study — simulate interview conditions.
Evening · ~1h
Review today's misses, re-solve one item from the re-do list, skim tomorrow's module titles. Stop by 21:30 — nothing learned after that survives the night anyway.
